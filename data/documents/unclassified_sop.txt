STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document ID: SOP-2024-001
Title: Daily Equipment Inspection Procedures
Effective Date: 2024-01-01

PURPOSE:
This SOP establishes standard procedures for daily equipment inspection to ensure operational readiness and safety.

SCOPE:
This procedure applies to all personnel responsible for equipment maintenance and operation.

PROCEDURES:

1. PRE-OPERATIONAL INSPECTION (0600-0630 Hours)
   1.1 Visual Inspection
       - Check all equipment for visible damage
       - Verify all safety guards are in place
       - Inspect cables and connections
       - Check for fluid leaks
   
   1.2 Power Systems
       - Test primary power supply
       - Verify backup power system status
       - Check battery charge levels
       - Test emergency power switch
   
   1.3 Communications
       - Test radio communications
       - Verify secure line functionality
       - Check network connectivity
       - Test emergency communication systems

2. OPERATIONAL CHECKS (During Operation)
   2.1 Performance Monitoring
       - Monitor system performance indicators
       - Log any anomalies or unusual behavior
       - Verify calibration accuracy
       - Check environmental conditions
   
   2.2 Safety Monitoring
       - Ensure personnel maintain safe distances
       - Monitor for warning indicators
       - Verify safety interlocks functional
       - Check emergency stop systems

3. POST-OPERATIONAL INSPECTION (1800-1830 Hours)
   3.1 Shutdown Procedures
       - Follow proper shutdown sequence
       - Secure all equipment
       - Store portable equipment properly
       - Lock access points
   
   3.2 Documentation
       - Complete daily log entries
       - Report any maintenance issues
       - Update equipment status board
       - File inspection checklist

4. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
   - All anomalies must be reported immediately
   - Daily inspection reports due by 0700 and 1900 hours
   - Maintenance requests submitted within 24 hours
   - Critical issues require immediate supervisor notification

5. RESPONSIBILITIES
   - Operators: Perform inspections, maintain logs
   - Supervisors: Review reports, authorize maintenance
   - Maintenance: Respond to issues, perform repairs
   - Command: Review trends, allocate resources

SAFETY NOTES:
- Always follow safety procedures
- Use appropriate personal protective equipment
- Never bypass safety interlocks
- Report unsafe conditions immediately

CLASSIFICATION: UNCLASSIFIED
Distribution: All Personnel
